Resources: Additional Data & Activism

Check out resources on police shootings in the U.S. Below are links to more data, and how you get take action and get involved. Click on the images to get more information.


Black Lives Matter

Black Lives Matter was founded in 2013 in response to the acquittal of Trayvon Martin’s murderer. It is a movement advocating for non-violent protest against incidents of police brutality and racially motivated violence against black people.






Campaign Zero

Campaign zero is a movement advocating for police reform in the United States. The campaign has proposed at least ten proposals for police reform including limiting the use of force and requiring police to wear body cameras while on duty.






Fatal Force: Police shootings database

The Washington Post's database contains records of every fatal shooting in the United States by a police officer in the line of duty since Jan. 1, 2015.The Post’s database is updated regularly as fatal shootings are reported and as facts emerge about individual cases.

FBI Use-of-Force Data

The FBI created the National Use of Force Data Collection in 2015, in partnership with law enforcement agencies, to provide nationwide statistics on law enforcement use-of-force incidents.
