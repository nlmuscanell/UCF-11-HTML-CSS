Fatality Rates by Victim Race (Based on Population Size)



Analysis: Because race is not equally represented in the U.S., I also calcuated rate ratios to account for differences in population sizes. The rates of fatality for each racial group were calculated by taking the number of victims in a group between 2015-2020, dividing it by the population estimate for that group, and then multiplying this value by 1,000. Thus, the rates presented are fatalites for each group, per 1,000 people.

This graph shows that when we account for population differences between racial groups, the incidence rate for fatalities is different than when looking at percentages. Here, we see that the rate of fatalities is highest for black people, whereas percentages showed that the highest percent of victims were White. These are both true. Most victims of police shootings are white, but this is largely due to the fact that the population of White people in the U.S. is much larger than the population of other ethnic groups. When we look at fatalities by accounting for population sizes, we see that even though most victims are white, black people have a higher fatality rate vs. white people.
